  • LMPD obtain no-knock warrant
    Mar 12th 2020

  • Louisville Metro Police Department wrote five affidavits seeking a judge's permission for no-knock searches, which were all approved within 12 minutes by Jefferson Circuit Judge Mary Shaw.

    - USA TODAY

  • Warrants related to an investigation of Jamarcus Glover and several others whom police suspected were dealing drugs out of a home at 2424 Elliott Ave. Three of the warrants were for properties on Elliott Avenue, one was for a home on Muhammad Ali Boulevard and the last was for Breonna Taylor's apartment on Springfield Drive -- 10 miles from the suspected drug house.

    - WLKY

  • LMPD suspected that Taylor was receiving packages on behalf of her ex-boyfriend, Jamarcus Glover, who police suspected of drug trafficking, according to court documents.

    - USA TODAY

  • The search warrant authorized the police to search Taylor's apartment, two vehicles, and three people, including Taylor, and to seize, among other things, drugs and drug paraphernalia, money, safes, weapons, documents and computers.

    The warrant did not specifically connect Taylor to any alleged drug activity.

    - CNN

  • Lawyers for Taylor's family allege in court documents filed in Jefferson Circuit Court...that a police squad — named Place-Based Investigations — had 'deliberately misled' narcotics detectives to target a home on Elliott Avenue, leading them to believe they were after some of the city's largest violent crime and drug rings.

    - Courier Journal

  • Sgt. Jon Mattingly and several others were briefed on the plan for the execution of the warrants. Mattingly later said in a police interview that he drove by Taylor's apartment around 11:30 p.m. to scope it out.

  • The Murder of Breonna Taylor
    Mar 13th

  • Officers began to bang on Taylor's door, setting into the motion the events that led to her death.

    - WHAS 11
    12:40 am

  • Ms. Taylor and her boyfriend, Kenneth Walker, had been in bed, but got up when they heard a loud banging at the door. Mr. Walker said he and Ms. Taylor both called out, asking who was at the door. Mr. Walker later told the police he feared it was Ms. Taylor’s ex-boyfriend trying to break in.

    - New York Times

  • Kenneth Walker, 27, says he woke up and believed someone was trying to break into the apartment. There was banging on the door, he says, but police never announced themselves. He reached for his licensed handgun after he and Taylor asked who it was and got no response... He fired one shot, hitting an officer in the leg.

    - Vox

  • After the police broke the door off its hinges, Mr. Walker fired his gun once, striking Sgt. Jonathan Mattingly in a thigh. The police responded by firing several shots, striking Ms. Taylor five times. One of the three officers on the scene, Detective Brett Hankison, who has since been fired, shot 10 rounds blindly into the apartment.

    - New York Times

  • Police then fired more than 20 rounds into the apartment. Taylor died at the scene. Walker was arrested and charged with attempted murder of a police officer and aggravated assault.

    Police found no drugs in the apartment, and both Taylor and Walker have no criminal history.

    - Vox

  • Taylor's neighbors began calling 911 to report shots fired. A minute later, officers on scene called dispatch saying an officer had been shot.
